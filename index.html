Academic Research Consulting & Data Science Services

Dawn Marie Daras provides advanced academic research consulting services specializing in statistical analysis, research design, data science modeling, and reproducible research workflows. Her work integrates behavioral science, quantitative methodology, and modern computational tools to support researchers, organizations, and institutions in producing rigorous, publishable results.

The core focus of this consulting practice is to bridge the gap between academic theory and applied data science. Many research projects fail not because of lack of data, but because of weak design, inconsistent methodology, or incorrect statistical interpretation. This consulting practice ensures that research is structurally sound, statistically valid, and reproducible across environments.

This service is designed for researchers, graduate students, academic institutions, policy analysts, and organizations working with complex datasets or research questions requiring statistical rigor.

  • Graduate students completing theses or dissertations

Statistical Tools & Technical Stack

Analyses are conducted using industry-standard statistical and data science tools. Each tool is selected based on the research problem, data structure, and required level of interpretability.

  • Python – pandas, NumPy, scikit-learn, statsmodels
  • R / RStudio – statistical modeling, visualization, and inference
  • SAS – enterprise-level statistical analysis and reporting
  • SPSS – behavioral and social science data analysis
  • SQL – data extraction, transformation, and integration

All workflows emphasize reproducibility, version control, and transparent methodology, ensuring that results can be independently validated and replicated.

Research Design Philosophy

Strong research begins long before analysis. Many analytic failures occur at the design stage, where variables are poorly defined, sampling strategies are weak, or hypotheses are not operationalized correctly.

This consulting approach prioritizes research architecture first: defining measurable constructs, aligning data collection methods with research questions, and ensuring that statistical models reflect the actual structure of the problem being studied.

By strengthening research design upfront, downstream analysis becomes more reliable, interpretable, and publishable.

Reproducible Research & Data Integrity

Reproducibility is a foundational principle of this consulting practice. Every analysis is designed so that results can be independently verified using the same dataset, code, and methodological framework.

This includes structured workflows in Python and R, documentation of statistical assumptions, and transparent reporting of model selection and evaluation criteria.

Reproducibility is not optional—it is a requirement for credible scientific and analytical work.

Machine Learning & Predictive Modeling

Machine learning is applied where appropriate to support prediction, classification, and pattern detection. Models are selected based on interpretability, data availability, and research constraints rather than complexity alone.

Techniques may include regression models, classification systems, clustering methods, and ensemble approaches depending on the research objective.

Research Workflow Example

A typical consulting engagement follows a structured workflow:

  1. Define research question and objectives
  2. Develop statistical and methodological design
  3. Prepare and clean dataset
  4. Select and validate analytical models
  5. Run statistical or machine learning analysis
  6. Interpret results and validate findings
  7. Deliver publication-ready report or presentation
